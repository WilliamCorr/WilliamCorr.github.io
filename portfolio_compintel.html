Artificial Intelligence Machine-Learning

. . .

Computational Intelligence and Artificial Intelligence is an area that I find very interesting, so much so I have decided to pursue a Masters in the topic. I had always pursued a personal interest in it as it is widely implemented in game design. I first implemented the A* algorithm for pathfinding enemies in a game development module, then onto a program in C that used parallel processing to imitate the processes of cellular automata similar to Conway's-the game of life. We continued to learn about state machines and decision trees and other properties related to areas in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. This led to more modules related to logical expressions and algorithm design, data mining, computer vision, and natural language processing. These complex areas tend to lean toward machine learning just by the sheer complexity of the data that is processed.

In my final year, more than half of my subjects contained material related to machine learning. The final individual project 'Machine Learning Augmented Reality Bicycle Safety Apllication' was powered by a Convolutional Neural Network made with TensorFlow and Keras. The process of data mining was used to find the right combination of data for training for good prediction. I continue to experiment with many machine learning libraries in personal projects and am learning extensively about this subject in my recent Masters degree.

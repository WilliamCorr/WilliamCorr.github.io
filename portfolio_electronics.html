Electronics & Microcontrollers

Arduino, NanoPCs, Raspberry Pi

My interest in electronics is probably a life long one. I would have an interest in ‘gadgets’ and want to know how they work. In the first year of university, we had a module based on computer architecture which included designing a whisker sensor vehicle with an Arduino kit to complete a maze. After that, I joined the engineering society where we would devise projects and discuss electronics and engineering concepts. During this time I took advantage to program any microcontroller creating sumo bots with IR sensors on programmable lily pads in C programming compiled into faster hex code.

I would go on to create many other projects in Arduino for demonstrative purposes in the time I lead the society which attracted a diverse range of new members. Electronics to me bridges into the world of computing where my degree is, having spent three years studying networking protocols in CISCO I find IOT projects interesting to implement. The potential for networking, computing, automation, and electronics when synergised creates a new kind of industrial revolution, which people are already beginning to implement.
